Social structuralism is the idea that people operate within pre-established systems. It takes a conscious effort to remove oneself from their immediate realm of understanding to see the greater context. Our perspectives are shaped by what we’ve been taught, whether through personal experiences or repeated patterns of behavior. These norms remain intact unless a shift in context forces those norms to be acknowledged.

The critique that people are unable process things beyond their immediate lens of understanding is a harsh truth to bear. This idea remains constant in abstrct culture. Our responses are direct reflections of our personal ideals and to acknowledge how they can hinder our ability to see things as they are.

Shift to Question features a web-based interactive and research journal that juxtaposes digital engagement with current issues. People can gain a greater understanding of how social structures are formed within their context by analyzing how people process and respond to prevalent topics.
